Therapeutic Massage for Recovery

Therapeutic massage at our studio is a wellness service focused on relaxation and recovery after everyday strain or exercise. Sessions are customized to your comfort level. Individual experiences vary; massage is not medical treatment.

Swedish massage uses long, flowing strokes to promote relaxation. Deep-tissue methods address chronic tightness in the neck, shoulders, and lower back. Sports massage may help active clients prepare for movement or unwind after workouts.

Lymphatic wellness massage uses gentle strokes that some clients find helpful for comfort and relaxation when legs feel heavy. This is a wellness service, not medical lymphatic treatment. Before a session, the therapist reviews areas of discomfort and contraindications to choose appropriate intensity and duration.

  • May ease everyday muscle tension
  • Supports mobility and flexibility as part of wellness care
  • Recovery-focused sessions after physical activity
  • Relaxation and stress relief for many clients

Aromatherapy and Sensory Recovery

Aromatherapy enhances the effects of massage through olfactory receptors directly linked to the brain's limbic system, which governs emotion and memory. A well-chosen blend of essential oils reduces anxiety, helps you relax more deeply, and shifts the body into recovery mode within the first minutes of a session.

Lavender and chamomile are traditionally used for evening treatments: they calm the nervous system and improve falling asleep. Eucalyptus and mint refresh breathing and ease muscle tension after workouts. Citrus notes вЂ” orange, grapefruit, lemon вЂ” provide a light energizing effect; they are chosen for morning sessions when you need to gently wake the body without overstimulation.

Essential oils are diluted in a carrier base at a safe concentration before skin application. We ask about allergies and scent preferences because client comfort is essential. Aromatherapy is used for relaxation and sensory wellness вЂ” essential oils are not intended to diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent disease.

After your session, you receive home-care recommendations: which oils to add to a bath, how to do a light foot self-massage before bed, and how to air the room after using an aroma diffuser. This extends the benefits of the treatment and turns recovery into a lasting habit, not a one-time event.
